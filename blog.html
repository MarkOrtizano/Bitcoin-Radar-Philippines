Bitcoin Radar - find location easily

Sung Choi from Coinme talking Bitcoin ATM Importance

The hosts of one of the only Bitcoin-related radio shows in America expressed concerns about how hard it is to buy Bitcoin and what hurdles you face when you go through exchanges. Since it’s always better to have different options for handling your crypto transactions, they decided to give a call to Sung Choi, the vice president of strategy and Business Development of Coinme – one of the prominent Bitcoin ATM company in the world.

The Advantages

Talking about the advantages of Bitcoin ATMs compared to exchanges, Sung went a step further talking about the advantages of using Coinme specifically. The main difference between them and the more traditional Bitcoin ATM companies is that Coinme offers an option to custody the funds on behalf of the user providing them with a more bank-like experience. This practically means that if anything goes wrong with your account Coinme will help you recover your credentials, which is especially useful to those customers that are not as tech-savvy. At the same time it brings additional risks, as users don’t control the funds, and the users can lose the funds like it happened with a recent FTX case.

Another thing that differentiates Coinme from others is its business model. Namely, they do not do business with individual store owners but only with larger enterprises. Their partnerships with Coinstar and MoneyGram enabled them instant access to 21000 machines worldwide. So, the benefit here is obvious. As Sung claims about 90% of Americans are within five minutes of a Coinme location. Further on, using the existing infrastructure provides their customers to consolidate the number of outlets that they use for their finances. In other words, you can handle your fiat or crypto transactions in one place.

User Experience

After you go to coinme.com and find the nearest location, you can go there and just put the money in and you will receive a voucher that you can redeem in your Coinme wallet. You can create the account before or after you generate the voucher, either way, it’s valid. Verifying your account should not take more than a couple of minutes and there are two different methods. You can use just your phone number and SSN, which will allow you to handle transactions up to $1,000. Or you can do a document verification where you submit an ID and a selfie. This will set you up for transactions up to $2,900. After you create the account and redeem the voucher, you can send your Bitcoin to any other address you like.

Coinme machines handle only cash for now and will charge an 11% fee on Coinstar locations, while MoneyGram locations have a lower fee of 4%. They also offer eight different assets other than Bitcoin.

The Community

Talking about the advantages of Bitcoin ATMs compared to exchanges, Sung went a step further talking about the advantages of using Coinme specifically. The main difference between them and the more Coinme is a proud community educator since 2014, helping the public understand how crypto works and what are the benefits. They even partnered with other companies and pushed out a campaign called crypto literacy where you can go and actually take a small test to see how crypto-literate you are. Sung also shared an interesting story from back in the day when they got a lot of calls from entrepreneurs wanting to know how crypto works since sellers from Alibaba asked them to pay in crypto because of huge PayPal fees. Having access to a huge number of locations through partnerships with Coinstar and MoneyGram allows Coinme the opportunity to gain insight into the demographic trends of the crypto community. Apart from the fact that most Bitcoin ATMs users are either unbanked or underbanked, they noticed an interesting rise in the number of generation Z customers meaning that a lot of younger people choose to stick with cash, rather than using the traditional banking system.
